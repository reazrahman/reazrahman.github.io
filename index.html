Hello!

Welcome! I am I K M Reaz Rahman, a Ph.D. student at the University of California, Berkeley, with an expected graduation date in Spring 2026. Prior to beginning my doctoral studies, I served as a Lecturer and Assistant Professor in the Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering (EEE) at the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET). I completed both my B.Sc. (2017) and M.Sc. (2021) in EEE from BUET.

Research Interest

My research interests center on the exploration of novel semiconductor materials at the nanoscale and their applications in solid-state electronics and optoelectronics. Currently, I am working on controlling spectrally pure single-photon emission from two-dimensional chalcogenides. Previously, my research focused on the aligned growth of one-dimensional tellurium using nanoscale confinement, enabling access to previously untapped quantum phenomena that remain elusive in bulk materials. This synthesis approach also reduces variability and allows coherent integration with silicon electronics at room temperature.

My prior experience includes enhancing the thermal stability of tellurium for use as a p-type channel material with feasible back-end-of-line integration. Before that, I investigated the effects of charging on core-shell quantum dots to understand the degradation mechanisms of these nanoparticles in light-emitting devices. Additionally, I studied electroluminescence in silicon—a material typically not known for this property due to its indirect bandgap. We uncovered that the observed electroluminescence originates from hot carrier effects, occurring when bipolar carriers are injected with high-slew-rate AC pulses.

Lead Projects

  • Single Photon Emission from 2D Chalcogenides
    Developing control over spectrally pure single photon emission in two-dimensional chalcogenide materials for applications in quantum communication and optoelectronics.
  • Aligned Growth of One-Dimensional Tellurium Molecular Wires
    Engineered methods for the controlled, aligned growth of tellurium nanowires using nanoscale confinement, unlocking access to novel quantum phenomena and simplifying silicon integration.
  • Thermally Stable Ruthenium Contacts for Tellurium Transistors
    Developed robust ruthenium contacts to enable reliable and stable operation of p-type tellurium transistors at elevated temperatures, advancing next-generation electronics manufacturing.
  • Gated Photoluminescence in Quantum Dot Thin Films
    Designed devices to study photoluminescence in gated quantum dot films, uncovering degradation pathways affecting the efficiency of core-shell nanocrystals.
  • Electroluminescence in Silicon Capacitors
    Uncovered mechanisms behind light emission in silicon under high-speed AC carrier injection.
